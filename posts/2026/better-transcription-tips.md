A few small habits during recording have a bigger effect on accuracy
than any model upgrade we can ship.

## 1. Record at 16kHz mono or higher

Phone defaults are fine; lossy compression below 64kbps starts to bite.
If your recorder offers WAV or FLAC, prefer it over MP3 for important
recordings.

## 2. One microphone per speaker, ideally

Two laptops in the same room beats one in the middle. If that's not
possible, put the recorder closer to the *quieter* speaker.

## 3. Tag the dialect if you know it

SMEAN auto-detects, but Battambang and Khmer Surin benefit from a hint.
Use the dialect dropdown when uploading.

## 4. Watch the background music

Subtitle-style overlays are fine; loud Khmer pop in the same channel
hurts. Run audio through a free noise-suppression tool first if needed.

## 5. Don't pre-translate code-switched meetings

Leave English where it is; SMEAN handles the boundary. Manually
substituting Khmer translations into English-spoken sentences confuses
both the language model and downstream readers.
